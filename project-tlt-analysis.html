Time-Series Analysis of 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF

Full Paper (PDF) →

Abstract

This paper analyzes the behavior of daily returns of the iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF (TLT), focusing on the impact of macroeconomic and financial market variables, including market volatility index, S&P500 returns, changes in Fed rates, Treasury bond market yield, and inflation. Models utilized daily data from January 2004 to July 2024, the analysis incorporates advanced time-series models to capture the dynamics of returns in TLT. An ARIMAX model with state-dependent dynamics using a Threshold Autoregressive (TAR) framework reveals the significant role of Federal Funds Rate changes in regime-dependent effects on returns. Additionally, a GARCH-ARIMAX model was employed to address volatility clustering and persistence, highlighting the impact of recent shocks and long-term volatility persistence. Both models demonstrate the critical influence of market volatility and equity returns on the daily performance of TLT. The findings provide a comprehensive understanding of the drivers of long-term Treasury bond returns and underscore the importance of accounting for regime changes and time-varying volatility in modeling.

Data

  • Asset: TLT daily returns (R_TLT)
  • Sample: Jan 5, 2004 – Jul 1, 2024 (5,156 trading-day observations)
  • Sources: Yahoo Finance + FRED (lower-frequency macro series mapped to daily frequency)
  • Key drivers used in core specs: VIX, S&P 500 returns (R_GSPC), inflation (INFL), long-term Treasury yield (DGS20), and changes in the Fed funds rate (dFEDRATE)

Methods

  • Baseline benchmark: ARIMA(2,0,2) selected by AIC (AIC = 13,765.91); ex-post RMSFE = 1.0598
  • Regime switching (preferred mean-dynamics extension): ARIMAX(2,0,2) + TAR, where regimes are defined by dFEDRATE (rate cuts vs stable/increasing-rate periods) and implemented via regime-specific lag interactions
    – Performance improvement vs baseline: AIC = 13,148.81; RMSFE = 0.9097872
  • Volatility modeling (best overall model in the writeup): GARCH(1,2) variance layered on an ARIMA(1,0,3) mean with exogenous drivers (VIX and lag, R_GSPC and lag, INFL, dFEDRATE, and lagged DGS20)
    – Strong ARCH effects: ARCH LM χ² = 725.141 (p = 0.0000)
    – Best fit + slightly better forecasting: AIC = 12,191.62; RMSFE = 0.90935889

Key Results

What the models agree on:

  • Flight-to-safety is the dominant daily story: VIX is a positive driver of TLT returns and S&P 500 returns are a negative driver, consistent across both the ARIMAX+TAR model and the GARCH-ARIMAX model.
  • Monetary policy regimes matter: the ARIMAX+TAR specification shows that TLT's lag dynamics differ materially during rate-cut regimes versus stable/increasing-rate regimes (dFEDRATE-based state interactions).
  • Volatility clustering is real (and large): the ARCH LM test strongly rejects no-ARCH, and the GARCH terms imply persistent volatility dynamics, meaning shocks propagate into future risk.
  • Adding regimes + time-varying volatility materially improves forecasting vs a plain ARIMA benchmark: RMSFE falls from 1.0598 (baseline ARIMA) to ~0.91 in both the TAR and GARCH specifications, with the GARCH-ARIMAX model also improving directional realism.

Conclusion

Across 2004–2024, TLT's daily returns are best explained by (1) risk-off conditions (higher VIX and weaker equity returns) and (2) monetary-policy regime shifts captured by changes in the Fed funds rate. Relative to a simple ARIMA benchmark, incorporating regime dependence and volatility dynamics substantially improves fit and forecasting accuracy, and the GARCH-ARIMAX specification provides the most realistic description of volatility behavior in TLT returns.
